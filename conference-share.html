    Research Overview

    I am currently a PhD researcher at the MXR (Mobility in eXtended reality) Lab at TU Delft, investigating the capabilities and differences of various XR technologies in pedestrian behavior studies across different contexts.

    My research focuses on:
    1. Comparative analysis of VR, AR, and MR technologies in transportation research
    2. Pedestrian behavior modeling and simulation in extended reality environments
    3. Human-Machine Interface design for mobility applications
    4. Behavioral science applications in transportation planning

    About MXR Lab

    The MXR (Mobility in eXtended reality) Labat TU Delft is a cutting-edge research facility dedicated to exploring the intersection of extended reality technologies and transportation planning. Our lab focuses on:
    • Developing immersive environments for transportation research
    • Studying human behavior in virtual and augmented reality settings
    • Creating innovative tools for urban planning and mobility design
    • Advancing the understanding of human-machine interaction in transportation

    Research Impact

    Our research contributes to:
    • Urban Planning: Better understanding of pedestrian behavior for safer city design
    • Technology Development: Improved XR interfaces for transportation applications
    • Policy Making: Data-driven insights for transportation infrastructure decisions
    • Accessibility: Inclusive design for diverse user groups
